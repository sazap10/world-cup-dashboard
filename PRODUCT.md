# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Football fans in the UK following the 2026 FIFA World Cup. They open the dashboard
between or before matches to answer fast questions: *What's on next? When does it
kick off in my time? Where do I watch it? How did the group end up? Who plays who in
the knockouts?* Context is glance-driven: phone on the sofa, laptop at work, often
checking a single fact and leaving.

## Product Purpose

A single-screen tournament companion for World Cup 2026. It collapses fixtures,
results, group tables, the knockout bracket, and UK broadcast information into one
fast, trustworthy surface. Success is the user getting their answer in one glance and
knowing exactly when and on which UK channel to watch the next game, in their own
timezone.

## Brand Personality

Confident, energetic, precise. The voice of a good matchday graphics package: bold
where it matters (the live game, the next kickoff), quiet everywhere else (tables,
metadata). Three words: **broadcast, kinetic, dependable.** It should feel like the
tournament is happening *now*.

## Anti-references

- Generic admin dashboard with identical KPI cards in a grid.
- Cluttered fantasy-football / betting-aggregator density and ad noise.
- Flat gray "data table dump" with no sense of occasion.
- Gradient-text hero metrics and decorative glassmorphism.

## Design Principles

1. **The live game is the loudest thing on screen.** Everything else defers to it.
2. **Time is personal.** Every kickoff renders in the user's chosen timezone, BST by default, with no mental arithmetic.
3. **Answer, then detail.** Lead with the headline (who, when, what channel); reveal depth on demand.
4. **Trust the numbers.** Standings, results, and tiebreakers are computed, never hand-waved, and the data's nature is stated honestly.
5. **Earned familiarity.** Standard, learnable patterns over invented affordances; the tool disappears into the task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, verified against the dark surface.
Match status is never conveyed by color alone (a LIVE label and pulse, W/D/L letters,
not just red/green). Full keyboard navigation and visible focus rings. Honors
`prefers-reduced-motion` (live pulse and reveals degrade to static/crossfade).
